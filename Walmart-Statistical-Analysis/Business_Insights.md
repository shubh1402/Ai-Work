# Business Insights & Recommendations

## Key Insights

### 1. Male Customers Showed Higher Average Spending
Analysis showed that male customers had a higher average purchase amount compared to female customers during Walmart Black Friday sales.

### 2. Confidence Intervals Did Not Significantly Overlap
The confidence interval analysis indicated statistically meaningful differences between male and female spending behavior.

### 3. Married Customers Demonstrated Strong Purchasing Behavior
Married customers showed relatively higher spending patterns, indicating stronger family-oriented purchasing behavior.

### 4. Age Groups Displayed Different Purchase Trends
Customer spending varied significantly across age groups, allowing opportunities for targeted marketing campaigns.

### 5. Purchase Distribution Was Right-Skewed
The purchase amount distribution showed the presence of high-value outliers, indicating premium customer segments.

---

# Business Recommendations

## 1. Gender-Based Marketing Campaigns
Walmart can design targeted Black Friday campaigns based on customer gender spending behavior.

## 2. Personalized Promotions
Use customer demographics such as age and marital status to provide personalized offers and discounts.

## 3. Loyalty Programs for High-Spending Customers
Introduce premium loyalty programs for customer segments demonstrating consistently high purchase behavior.

## 4. Family-Oriented Bundles
Since married customers exhibit stronger spending behavior, Walmart can launch family-focused product bundles and promotional offers.

## 5. Age-Specific Product Recommendations
Use age-based customer segmentation to improve recommendation systems and increase customer engagement.

# Statistical Conclusion

The Central Limit Theorem and confidence interval analysis demonstrated statistically significant differences in spending patterns across customer demographics.

This project highlights how statistical inference can support business decision-making and customer behavior analysis in large-scale retail environments.
